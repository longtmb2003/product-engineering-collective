# Content Bible

## Tone

Professional.

Calm.

Confident.

Human.

Never arrogant.

---

## Voice

Write like Linear.

Not like a software agency.

Avoid marketing clichés.

Every sentence should feel intentional.

---

## Never say

World-class

Best

Leading

Expert team

Top-quality

Innovative solutions

Cutting-edge

Trusted by...

Award-winning

Passionate developers

Rockstar engineers

---

## Prefer

Reliable

Thoughtful

Intentional

Carefully built

Production-ready

Maintainable

Scalable

Collaborative

Ownership

Engineering quality

---

## Headlines

Short.

Usually 3–7 words.

Examples

Turn ideas into products.

Engineering without handoffs.

Built with ownership.

Software that lasts.

---

## Paragraphs

Maximum 3 sentences.

Avoid walls of text.

---

## Buttons

Prefer verbs.

Start a Conversation

See Our Work

Meet the Team

View Product

---

## Team Cards

Never list every technology.

Explain

what this person helps clients achieve.

Bad

Backend Developer

Java

Spring

Kafka

Redis

Oracle

Docker

Good

Designs reliable backend systems that continue to work as products grow.

---

## Product Cards

Always answer

Why does this product matter?

instead of

Which technologies were used?

---

## Sections

Every section should answer one question.

Hero

Who are we?

Problem

Why us?

Products

Can we trust you?

Team

Who builds?

Process

How do you work?

CTA

What's next?

---

## General Rule

If a sentence sounds like marketing,

rewrite it.

If it sounds like engineering,

keep it.
